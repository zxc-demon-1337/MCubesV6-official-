Setting the corners of the second layer

At this stage, you need to place the corners of the second layer correctly. So that the colors on each of the upper elements, except for yellow, match the colors of the elements below them. To do this, you need to find the yellow-red-green corner and put it in the right place.: above the other corner on the first layer with red and green color. It can be deployed in different ways, the main thing is that it stands in the right place.
R - turn the right side clockwise
R - turn the right side clockwise
R - turn the right side clockwise
Next, without rotating the faces of the cube, we inspect the top layer and find other corners that stand correctly. That is, the colors on them (except yellow) should be the same as the colors at the corners that stand under them. Two correctly positioned corners can stand side by side or diagonally.
R - turn the right side clockwise
R - turn the right side clockwise
If there are no more such corners, you need to take any other corner on the top layer and put it in the correct place. Next, we check the other corners again. We need to find a situation where at least two corners are in the right places. If they are standing next to each other, you need to take the cube so that the correct corners are on the left and run the algorithm. If we run the algorithm diagonally from any position, then we get the first situation where we need to place the 2 correct corners on the left and do the algorithm again.
